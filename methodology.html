Methodology

MarginSignal OS measures eight operational indices. Each one tracks a specific dimension of resolution economics. Here is what they measure and why they matter.

ECI

Engagement Coherence Index

Composite health score across all operational dimensions. ECI is the single number that answers “how healthy is this operation?” Ranges from 0 to 100. Healthy operations score 65+. Distressed operations score below 50. Calculated from the weighted combination of all other indices.

CPR

Cost Per Resolution

The fully loaded cost to resolve a single customer issue to completion — including all contacts, transfers, callbacks, escalations, and channel switches. Unlike Cost Per Call, CPR measures outcome economics. The gap between CPC and CPR is typically $35+ and represents structural margin waste.

ERS

Escalation Resolution Score

Measures whether escalations produce resolutions or bounces. A high ERS means escalations are working — complex issues are reaching the right people and getting resolved. A low ERS means escalations are a cost multiplier without a resolution outcome. Directly tied to the Escalation Bounce fault line.

R7

Repeat Contact Rate (7-Day Window)

Percentage of customer issues that generate a repeat contact within 7 days. Industry average: 20–30%. Every repeat doubles the resolution cost. R7 is the purest measure of first-contact resolution failure — more honest than FCR because it tracks at the issue level, not the contact level.

TDI

Transfer Depth Index

Average number of handoffs per resolution. A TDI of 1.0 means most issues resolve on first contact. A TDI of 2.5+ means the average issue bounces through 2–3 agents before resolution. Each additional transfer adds 40–60% to resolution cost and drops CSAT by 10–15 points.

EDR

Escalation Depth Ratio

Ratio of escalated contacts to total contacts, weighted by resolution outcome at the escalated tier. EDR distinguishes between necessary escalations (complex issues that require specialist resolution) and unnecessary escalations (issues that could have been resolved at Tier 1 with better tools or authority).

RCEI

Resolution Cost Efficiency Index

Measures how efficiently the operation converts spend into resolved outcomes. RCEI compares actual resolution cost against the theoretical minimum cost (if every issue resolved on first contact with no transfers, repeats, or escalations). The gap between actual and theoretical is your recoverable margin.

DLI

Demand Leakage Index

Percentage of total contact volume that is failure demand — contacts that exist only because a prior contact failed to resolve the issue. Industry range: 30–60% of all volume is failure demand. DLI quantifies the share of your operation that is paying to handle its own mistakes.

Methodology Note

All indices are calculated from operational data — ticket records, interaction logs, and resolution outcomes. No surveys. No interviews. No self-reported metrics. The diagnostic runs on anonymized CSV exports from your existing platforms (ConnectWise, Autotask, ServiceNow, Freshdesk, or custom CSV).

Index calculations reference established frameworks from COPC, ICMI, HDI, and published research from Deloitte and McKinsey. MarginSignal OS does not claim to have invented these measurement concepts. We have built a system that computes them forensically from operational data — which is something no BI tool or CCaaS platform does out of the box.
